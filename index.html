Why Praxis

Applied practice, not abstract strategy

AI does not create business value because an organization launches another pilot or buys another tool. It creates value when strategy changes how the organization works.

Praxis means applied practice. Fabro Praxis helps leaders translate AI opportunity into operating models, workflow redesign, governance, adoption plans, and measurable outcomes.

How We Work

From diagnosis to results

A structured four-phase process that builds organizational confidence alongside the strategy itself.

01

Diagnose

Assess current state: AI maturity, data readiness, existing workflows, team capabilities, and where value is being left on the table.

02

Prioritize

Map high-value use cases against organizational readiness. Separate the pilots worth scaling from the ones to leave behind.

03

Build the roadmap: governance, vendor decisions, process changes, change management, and the metrics that prove it's working.

04

Execute

Transition from plan to operation with the team equipped, workflows redesigned, and leadership aligned to sustain the change.

Operations · AI-ENABLED PLATFORM OPERATIONS

AI-assisted service transformation for a regulated edge platform

Led the redesign of post-sales operations for a regulated technology platform serving 1,200+ public-sector and enterprise customers across 3,000+ connected edge devices. Rebuilt the operating cadence across support, onboarding, field operations, customer success, hardware fulfillment, and platform operations, while introducing AI-assisted triage, operational telemetry, escalation governance, and executive KPI visibility.

89% reduction in MTTR, 75% reduction in support backlog, 92% CSAT, and connected-device uptime improved from 67% to 92%
OPERATIONS · SERVICE ECONOMICS

Turning a service-heavy program into a scalable operating model

Inherited a labor-intensive public-sector operations program with high onsite dependency, margin pressure, and inconsistent execution visibility. Redesigned the service model, introduced automation-assisted classification workflows, reduced unnecessary field intervention, and established a more predictable governance and reporting cadence across operations, finance, and customer-facing teams.

Service margin improved from approximately 15% to 70%, onsite visits reduced by 70%, and manual classification effort reduced by 60%
